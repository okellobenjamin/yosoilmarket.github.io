The Problem

Why Uganda's Agriculture Sector Needs Innovation

Agriculture is the backbone of Uganda's economy, employing over 70% of the population and contributing approximately 24% to the GDP (Uganda Bureau of Statistics, 2023). However, the sector faces significant challenges:

Critical Soil Degradation

According to the National Environment Management Authority (NEMA), Uganda loses approximately 80,000 hectares of fertile soil annually due to degradation. The FAO estimates that 45% of Uganda's arable land shows signs of soil degradation and nutrient depletion, directly impacting agricultural productivity.

Limited Access to Soil Testing

Only 4% of Ugandan farmers have ever tested their soil (Ministry of Agriculture, Animal Industry and Fisheries, 2023). The average distance to the nearest soil testing facility is 78 kilometers, making these services inaccessible to most smallholder farmers.

Declining Crop Yields

Studies by Makerere University's College of Agricultural Sciences show that soil nutrient deficiencies have led to a 12-15% decline in crop yields over the past decade. This translates to approximately UGX 1.8 trillion ($500 million) in lost agricultural output annually.

Our Solution

Real-time soil analysis at your fingertips

YoSoil is an innovative mobile application that provides real-time soil nutrient analysis, helping Ugandan farmers make informed decisions about fertilizer application and crop management. Unlike existing solutions that require expensive laboratory testing or rely on outdated regional soil maps, YoSoil delivers personalized recommendations based on actual conditions in a farmer's field.

1

Soil Sampling

Farmers collect small soil samples from their fields using the simple sampling kit provided with the app.

2

Analyze with App

Using the YoSoil app and camera on their smartphone, farmers can instantly analyze key soil nutrients including nitrogen, phosphorus, potassium, and moisture content.

3

Get Recommendations

Receive personalized fertilizer recommendations and soil management practices tailored to your soil conditions and the crops you're growing.

4

Purchase Fertilizers

Access approved fertilizers directly through the YoSoil Marketplace, where we connect you with verified suppliers of recommended products.

What Makes YoSoil Different?

Affordable Analysis

Traditional soil testing in Uganda costs 75,000-150,000 UGX ($20-40) per sample. YoSoil reduces this cost by 80% while providing faster results.

Real-Time Results

Unlike conventional soil testing that takes 7-14 days for results, YoSoil provides analysis within minutes, allowing for immediate decision-making.

Location-Specific

Unlike general soil maps that provide broad recommendations, YoSoil gives field-specific guidance based on your exact location and soil conditions.

SDG Alignment

Contributing to sustainable development in Uganda

YoSoil directly contributes to several of the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals, with a particular focus on addressing Uganda's specific agricultural challenges:

SDG 2: Zero Hunger

By optimizing fertilizer use and improving soil health, YoSoil helps increase crop yields by up to 42%, directly addressing food security in Uganda where 41% of people are undernourished according to the World Food Programme.

SDG 13: Climate Action

Proper soil management can sequester up to 2-3 tons of carbon per hectare annually. With YoSoil's recommendations, Ugandan farmers can contribute to climate change mitigation while improving their yields.

SDG 15: Life on Land

NEMA reports that 70% of Uganda's agricultural lands show some form of degradation. YoSoil promotes sustainable land management practices that can reverse this trend and restore ecosystem health.

SDG 1: No Poverty

With the average Ugandan smallholder farmer earning just $2-3 per day, optimizing inputs can increase income by 25-35%. The Agricultural Sector Strategic Plan identifies improved inputs as key to poverty reduction.

Our Collaboration Story

The YoSoil team came together through a science innovation competition at Trinity College Nabbingo in 2023. Our diverse backgrounds in agriculture, technology, economics, and design allowed us to approach the challenge of soil health from multiple perspectives.

After extensive field research with farmers across Uganda and collaboration with the Faculty of Agriculture at Makerere University, we developed and refined the YoSoil technology to ensure it meets the specific needs of Ugandan farmers.
